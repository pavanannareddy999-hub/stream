Before you submit

Manuscript checklist

  • Manuscript in PDF or DOCX, with figures embedded or attached separately at full resolution.
  • Structured abstract of 250 words or fewer.
  • All author names, affiliations, and ORCID iDs (where available).
  • Data and code availability statement, including a link to any public repository.
  • Conflict of interest and funding disclosure.
  • Suggested reviewers are optional but help speed up assignment.
Formatting

Format standards

  • References in any consistent style at submission — reformatting happens after acceptance.
  • No strict page or word limit at initial submission; journals may request trimming before publication.
  • Figures should be legible at 100% zoom and include descriptive captions.
  • Supplementary material is submitted as a separate file and linked from the main text.
What to expect

FAQ

Common questions from authors.

Can I submit to a specific journal, or does Stream assign one for me?

Both work. Choose a journal directly if you know the right fit, or submit generally and an editor will route it based on scope — you'll be notified either way before review starts.

Is there a submission fee?

Publication fees apply only on acceptance, and fee waivers are available by default for authors without institutional funding — no separate application needed.

What happens if my paper is declined?

You'll receive the reviewer reports and can request a transfer to a different journal in the network without resubmitting from scratch.

Can I present at a conference before publication?

Yes — many authors present work-in-progress at Stream conferences while it's still under review. Let the conference organizers know the manuscript's status when you apply to speak.

Who owns the copyright?

You do. All Stream journals publish under an open-access license that keeps copyright with the authors.
