About Stream

Research shouldn't wait for
a quarterly issue.

Stream was built on a simple observation: the pace of review and publication had stopped matching the pace of research itself. So we rebuilt it.

Our story

Founded by editors who were tired of the backlog.

Stream started in 2018 as a single journal — Stream Cognitive Science — founded by a group of editors who had spent years watching strong papers sit in queues for issue slots that had nothing to do with their quality or readiness.

The fix wasn't a faster version of the old model. It was a different one: review continuously, publish the moment a paper clears it, and stop treating "the issue" as a meaningful unit of scholarly time.

From one journal to a network

Other editorial teams asked to run their journals the same way. Rather than build one infrastructure per journal, we built shared review tooling and a shared submission portal — and Stream became a network. Today it hosts 41 journals across the sciences, social sciences, and humanities, each with independent editorial control.

Why conferences too

Publishing continuously exposed a second problem: annual conferences were just as disconnected from when a field actually had something to discuss. Stream conferences are scheduled around active research fronts identified from submission activity across the network, not a fixed yearly slot.

What we hold constant

Editorial independence

Every journal sets its own scope, editorial board, and acceptance standards. Stream provides infrastructure, not editorial direction.

Review that doesn't batch

Manuscripts are assigned to reviewers as they arrive. Nothing sits in a queue waiting for an issue to fill up.

Open by default

All Stream journals publish open access. Authors keep copyright; readers never hit a paywall.

What we optimize for

Three commitments that shape every decision.

Speed with rigor

Fast review only counts if it's still thorough. Reviewers are matched by expertise, not availability alone, and every paper gets a minimum of two independent reviews.

Global reach

Authors and reviewers span 118 countries. Fee waivers are available by default for researchers without institutional funding.

Plain infrastructure

One submission portal, one formatting standard, one set of author tools — regardless of which journal or conference you're working with.

Editorial leadership

The people setting standards across the network.

Each journal has its own board; this group sets shared policy on review, ethics, and open access.
