How events get scheduled

Three signals decide when and where a conference happens.

01

Submission volume

A visible rise in submissions to a topic signals a field is active enough to warrant a gathering.

02

Editor nomination

Journal editors can nominate a topic directly when they see a cluster of related, high-quality work.

03

Author interest

Authors publishing on Stream can request a venue; enough requests on one topic can trigger scheduling.

04

Confirmed & opened

Once scheduled, the call for talks and posters opens to authors across the relevant journals first.

Want to present at a Stream conference?

Speaking slots are drawn primarily from authors publishing in the related journals.
